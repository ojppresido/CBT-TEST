Mathematical Expressions Demo

This page demonstrates proper formatting for all types of mathematical expressions using MathJax.

Fractions

Basic fraction: $\frac{3}{4}$

Complex fraction: $\frac{3^{-1} \times 9^{\frac{1}{2}}}{27^{-\frac{1}{3}}}$

Continued fraction: $\frac{1}{2 + \frac{1}{3 + \frac{1}{4}}}$

LaTeX: $\frac{3^{-1} \times 9^{\frac{1}{2}}}{27^{-\frac{1}{3}}}$

Exponentials

Basic exponent: $x^2$

Exponential function: $e^{x}$

Fractional exponent: $x^{\frac{1}{2}}$

Negative exponent: $3^{-1}$

Complex exponent: $e^{x^2 + 2x + 1}$

LaTeX: $e^{x^2 + 2x + 1}$

Roots

Square root: $\sqrt{16}$

Cube root: $\sqrt[3]{27}$

Fourth root: $\sqrt[4]{x^2 + 1}$

LaTeX: $\sqrt[4]{x^2 + 1}$

Logarithms

Natural logarithm: $\ln(x)$

Log base 10: $\log(x)$

Log with base: $\log_2(x)$

LaTeX: $\log_a(b)$

Trigonometric Functions

Sine: $\sin(x)$

Cosine: $\cos(x)$

Tangent: $\tan(x)$

Inverse sine: $\arcsin(x)$

Hyperbolic sine: $\sinh(x)$

Power of sine: $\sin^2(x)$

LaTeX: $\sin^2(x) + \cos^2(x) = 1$

Matrices

2x2 matrix: $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$

Determinant: $\det\begin{vmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{vmatrix}$

LaTeX: $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$

Summations and Integrals

Summation: $\sum_{i=1}^{n} i$

Infinite series: $\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^k}{k!}$

Product: $\prod_{i=1}^{n} x_i$

Integral: $\int_a^b f(x) dx$

Double integral: $\iint_D f(x,y) dx dy$

LaTeX: $\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx = \sqrt{\pi}$

Greek Letters

Lowercase: $\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta, \epsilon, \zeta, \eta, \theta, \iota, \kappa, \lambda, \mu, \nu, \xi, \pi, \rho, \sigma, \tau, \upsilon, \phi, \chi, \psi, \omega$

Uppercase: $A, B, \Gamma, \Delta, E, Z, H, \Theta, I, K, \Lambda, M, N, \Xi, \Pi, P, \Sigma, T, \Upsilon, \Phi, X, \Psi, \Omega$

LaTeX: $\varepsilon$ (variant epsilon), $\vartheta$ (variant theta), $\varpi$ (variant pi), $\varrho$ (variant rho), $\varsigma$ (variant sigma), $\varphi$ (variant phi)

Inequalities

Less than: $a < b$

Greater than: $a > b$

Less than or equal: $a \leq b$

Greater than or equal: $a \geq b$

Not equal: $a \neq b$

Approximately: $a \approx b$

Equivalent: $a \equiv b$

Proportional: $a \propto b$

LaTeX: $a \leq b \leq c$

Grouping and Parentheses

Auto-sizing: $\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)$

Square brackets: $\left[\frac{a}{b}\right]$

Curly braces: $\left\{\frac{a}{b}\right\}$

LaTeX: $\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)$

Complex Expressions

Quadratic formula: $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$

Euler's formula: $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$

Sine addition: $\sin(\alpha + \beta) = \sin\alpha \cos\beta + \cos\alpha \sin\beta$

Complex fraction from your example: $\frac{3^{-1} \times 9^{\frac{1}{2}}}{27^{-\frac{1}{3}}}$

LaTeX: $\frac{3^{-1} \times 9^{\frac{1}{2}}}{27^{-\frac{1}{3}}}$

Complete Example: Your Original Expression

Original expression: $\frac{3^{-1} \times 9^{\frac{1}{2}}}{27^{-\frac{1}{3}}}$

Let's simplify it step by step:

1. $3^{-1} = \frac{1}{3}$

2. $9^{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{9} = 3$

3. $27^{-\frac{1}{3}} = \frac{1}{27^{\frac{1}{3}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt[3]{27}} = \frac{1}{3}$

So the expression becomes: $\frac{\frac{1}{3} \times 3}{\frac{1}{3}} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{3}} = 3$

Result: $\frac{3^{-1} \times 9^{\frac{1}{2}}}{27^{-\frac{1}{3}}} = 3$
